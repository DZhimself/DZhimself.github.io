introduction to calculus and basic osciliation

In the first four days, the professor gives lecture about calculus and basic physics knowledge about osciliation. Before starting any labwork, the professor gives one lecture to give us basic idea about how to deal with errors and inaccuracy by matplotlib, which are also of great use to the later labwork.

labwork1: mass and period

This labwork is to determine the mass of a bob has nothing to do with the period of a pendulum system


$$
T=2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}
$$

  • experiment procedure
    • Hang weights of different masses on the rope
    • Swing the rope and use the stop watch to record each time The period of a simple pendulum
    • Draw a table to record the corresponding weight mass and pendulum cycle
  • Methods of controlling experimental errors
    • 1.Fix one end of the rope to ensure that the length of the rope remains the same for each experiment.
    • 2.Mark the position of the first swing to ensure that the amplitude is the same every time the experiment starts to swing.
    • 3.Measure the total time of multiple swings each time to reduce the error caused by human reaction time.
    • 4.Each measurement is repeated 5 times and averaged.

labwork2: amplitude of a lightly damped oscillator


$$
\frac{d^2x}{dt^2}+2\beta\omega_0\frac{dx}{dt}+\omega^2_0x=0
$$

$$
\beta=\frac{c}{2\sqrt{mk}}
$$

  • Plan

    • Measure the amplitude of an oscillating pendulum for 20 oscillations to find gamma and b.

  • method of measurement

    • Attach protractor and string with weight to a desk
    • Start the stopclock before releasing pendulum to avoid time error
    • Release pendulum at 10˚ to the left of 90˚ and stop it after 10 periods have passed
    • Record the process and create a table with amplitude and time
